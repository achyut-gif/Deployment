Kathmandu Hosts Annual Charity Run to Support Local Causes

Recent Incident in Kathmandu

Kathmandu, Nepal – On Sunday, February 16, over 800 participants gathered at the Narayanhiti Palace Museum grounds for Kathmandu’s annual charity run, raising funds for local organizations focused on education, healthcare, and poverty alleviation.

The event featured a 5K run and a 2K walk, with participants ranging from school children to senior citizens. Organizers reported that the charity run successfully raised NPR 1,000,000, which will be donated to various Nepali NGOs working on community development projects across the Kathmandu Valley.

This event highlights the spirit of giving within our city, and we are grateful for everyone’s participation,” said Rina Shrestha, the event coordinator. “The funds raised will directly benefit those who need it most, and it’s heartwarming to see so many come together for such an important cause.

Along with the run, the event included cultural performances, local food stalls, and wellness activities, creating a festive atmosphere for families. The Kathmandu Metropolitan City plans to make this event an annual tradition to foster a sense of community and support for social causes.
